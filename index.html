About

  Terra Sztain recieved her PhD in Chemistry from the University of California San Diego in 2021. As an NSF Graduate Research Fellow and Alfred P. Sloan Scholar, she performed interdisciplinary research under the advisement of Profs. Michael D. Burkart and J. Andrew McCammon. This research involved iterating between NMR spectroscopy and conventional and enhanced sampling molecular dynamics simulations to re-engineer biosynthetic pathways, and elucidate the mechanisms of SARS-CoV-2 viral proteins.

  She received her B.S. in Biochemistry from the University of California, Los Angeles in 2016, graduating cum laude. As an undergraduate she researched cancer epigenetics with Dr. Helena Chang at UCLA and with Dr. Dan Raz at City of Hope as a National Cancer Institute CURE Fellow.
